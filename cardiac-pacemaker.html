Cardiac Pacemaker Safety & Evolution

Electrical Safety Testing and Design Considerations

Overview

This project investigates the evolution of pacemakers from basic analog pulse generators to advanced programmable devices. It emphasizes the importance of safety in biomedical devices, with a specific focus on pacemakers that operate directly with heart tissue.

What is a Cardiac Pacemaker?

A cardiac pacemaker is a medical device implanted under the chest skin to regulate abnormal heart rhythms. It uses electrical pulses to prompt the heart to beat at a normal rate. Pacemakers are used in conditions such as bradycardia (slow heart rate) or heart block.

Objectives

  • Understand the historical and technological evolution of cardiac pacemakers.
  • Analyze the design criteria from a biomedical engineering perspective.
  • Perform safety testing procedures for electrical parameters in implantable devices.
  • Evaluate validation and regulatory testing standards (e.g., ISO, IEC).

Key Topics Covered

  • Types of pacemakers and their mechanisms
  • Electrical stimulation principles
  • Testing and validation of pacing circuits
  • Failure modes and patient safety protocols
  • Ethical and regulatory standards in device approval

Outcomes

  • Developed a simulation model for cardiac pulse delivery
  • Tested electrode materials and insulation quality
  • Proposed a risk mitigation protocol for current leakage
