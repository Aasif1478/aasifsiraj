Cardiac Pacemaker Safety & Evolution

Electrical Safety Testing and Design Considerations

Overview

This project investigates the evolution of pacemakers from basic analog pulse generators to advanced programmable devices. It emphasizes the importance of safety in biomedical devices, with a specific focus on pacemakers that operate directly with heart tissue.

What is a Cardiac Pacemaker?

A cardiac pacemaker is a small, battery-powered device implanted under the skin, usually near the collarbone, to manage irregular heart rhythms. It sends electrical impulses to stimulate the heart to beat at a normal rate.

There are different types of pacemakers:

  • Single-chamber pacemaker: Uses one lead placed in the right atrium or right ventricle.
  • Dual-chamber pacemaker: Uses two leads placed in the right atrium and right ventricle to coordinate contractions.
  • Biventricular pacemaker (CRT): Stimulates both ventricles to treat heart failure.

Pacemakers have evolved from fixed-rate analog pulse generators to sophisticated programmable digital devices. Modern pacemakers can adjust pacing rates based on activity, record data, and communicate wirelessly with external devices for monitoring.
